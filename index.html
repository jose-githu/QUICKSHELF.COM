Questions

Common Questions

Everything you need to know about the Quickshelf Gateway model.

What makes a product "KIMANA READY"? +
KIMANA READY ⚡ means the product is physically stocked by a verified local vendor in or around Kimana Town. These items can be delivered same-day within 15km, paid via M-Pesa, and sourced through a real local relationship — not shipped from a warehouse in Nairobi.
How does the Director Protocol choose which product to show first? +
When you search, Quickshelf checks the local PocketBase inventory first. If a local vendor has that product in stock, it appears at the top with the KIMANA READY badge. Jumia and Kilimall results fill the remaining catalogue. Local always wins placement.
Can local vendors import products from Jumia or Kilimall? +
Yes — the Vendor Dashboard includes a Sourcing Bridge. Vendors can browse the mirrored Jumia/Kilimall catalogue and click "Import" to add items to their own store. The system automatically applies a configurable price margin (default: base price + 15%).
What payment methods are supported? +
M-Pesa STK Push is the primary payment rail. Cash on delivery is also available for local orders. Future payment rails include stablecoin (USDC/USDT) for cross-border transactions as part of the broader Economic OS roadmap.
How do I track my order? +
Every order receives a unique ID. You can track status in real-time via your Customer Dashboard. Status flows: Pending → Confirmed → Delivered. You'll also get WhatsApp notifications for status changes on local orders.
